Festivals & Culture

Experience the vibrant Regada Water Festival, Mardicas dance, and celebrate the living Caviteño spirit.

Regada Water Festival

Celebrated every June 24 to honor St. John the Baptist, the Regada is one of the most joyful festivals in Cavite. Locals and tourists drench each other with water in the streets—a tradition tied to the saint's rite of baptism.

Pahimis Coffee Festival (Amadeo)

Held annually, this festival celebrates Amadeo's identity as a coffee capital. It features barista competitions, coffee tasting, cultural performances, and a grand street parade of coffee-themed floats.

Independence Day Celebration (Kawit)

Every June 12, Kawit holds the most significant Independence Day commemoration in the country, with a reenactment of the First Cry and the waving of the flag at the Aguinaldo Shrine balcony.

Local Barrio Fiestas

Every municipality in Cavite celebrates its patron saint's fiesta with unique traditions, street dances, and the famous Mardicas folk dance—a vibrant expression of Caviteño cultural heritage.
