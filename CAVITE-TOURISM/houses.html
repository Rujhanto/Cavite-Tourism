Ancestral Houses

Step back in time and admire the elegance of Cavite's heritage homes and colonial architecture.

Cuenca Ancestral House (Bacoor)

Famous for being the "Bahay Pamahalaan" or the seat of the Revolutionary Government under Emilio Aguinaldo in 1898. It features classic "Bahay na Bato" architecture with large Capiz windows and intricate woodwork.

Ladislao Diwa Shrine (Cavite City)

The ancestral home of one of the founders of the Katipunan. This house has been restored to preserve the memory of the revolution, housing personal artifacts and documents from the Katipunan era.

General Trias Heritage Houses

The town center of General Trias (formerly San Francisco de Malabon) is home to several private ancestral homes that have stood for over a century, showcasing the affluent history of the region's landholders.

Baldomero Aguinaldo Shrine (Binakayan)

The home of the cousin of Emilio Aguinaldo and a general in his own right. This two-story residence is a prime example of late 19th-century architecture, complete with a traditional "azotea" and wide wooden floorboards.
