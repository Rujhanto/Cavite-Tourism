Historical Landmarks

Explore the "Historical Capital of the Philippines" and walk through the cradle of the revolution.

Aguinaldo Shrine (Kawit)

This is the ancestral home of Emilio Aguinaldo, the first president of the Philippines. It was on the balcony of this house where the Philippine Independence was proclaimed on June 12, 1898.

Corregidor Island

Known as "The Rock," this fortress island served as a key defense during World War II. Visitors can explore ruins, the Malinta Tunnel, and various Pacific War memorials.

Bonifacio Trial House (Maragondon)

The Teodorico Reyes House is where the military trial of Andres Bonifacio was held in 1897. It now serves as a museum dedicated to the life and trials of the Supremo.

Battle of Binakayan Monument

This site commemorates the 1896 victory of Filipino revolutionaries against Spanish forces. It remains a symbol of Caviteño bravery and strategic military success.
