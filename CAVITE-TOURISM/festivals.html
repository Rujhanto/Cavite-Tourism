Local Festivals

Experience the soul of Cavite through its rhythmic dances, colorful costumes, and joyous community spirit.

Pahimis Festival (Amadeo)

A thanksgiving festival for a bountiful coffee harvest. "Pahimis" is a local word for "thanksgiving." The celebration features street dancing, coffee exhibitions, and free coffee tasting for all visitors.

Regada Water Festival (Cavite City)

Held every June, this is a week-long celebration in honor of St. John the Baptist. It culminates in a massive street party where residents and tourists are doused with water to symbolize blessings and purification.

Tahong Festival (Bacoor)

Bacoor celebrates its primary product—mussels. The festival features a culinary contest where various mussel-based dishes are showcased, alongside a colorful parade that highlights the city's coastal heritage.

Mardicas Festival (Ternate)

A unique war dance festival honoring the Sto. Niño. It commemorates the "Mardicas" (men of sea), the ancestors of Ternateños who migrated from Indonesia, featuring traditional costumes and rhythmic stick-fighting dances.
