About the Province

A land of heroes, highlands, and heritage located just a stone's throw away from the nation's capital.

Geography & Climate

Cavite is situated on the southern shores of Manila Bay. It is characterized by a rolling terrain that transitions from the coastal lowlands of Bacoor and Cavite City to the breezy, cool highlands of Tagaytay and Alfonso. This diverse geography allows visitors to enjoy both sunny beach days and chilly mountain evenings in a single trip.

The Historical Capital

Known as the "Historical Capital of the Philippines," Cavite was the primary theater of the 1896 Philippine Revolution. It is home to the site where the Philippine flag was first unfurled and where national independence was proclaimed in 1898. Every corner of the province tells a story of bravery and the birth of a nation.

Economy & Culture

Today, Cavite is one of the most industrialized and fastest-growing provinces in the country. Despite its rapid urbanization, it has successfully preserved its agricultural roots, remaining a top producer of world-class coffee, pineapple, and fresh seafood. The culture is a vibrant blend of traditional Hispanic influences and modern Filipino lifestyle.

Quick Facts

Region: CALABARZON (Region IV-A)
Capital: Imus (Official) / Trece Martires (Seat of Government)
Language: Tagalog, English, Chabacano
Major Industries: Tourism, Agriculture, Manufacturing
