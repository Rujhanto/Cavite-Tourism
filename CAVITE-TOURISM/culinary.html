Culinary Trails

Take a flavorful journey through the kitchens of Cavite and taste history in every bite.

Pancit Estacion (Cavite City)

A unique Caviteño noodle dish that uses glass noodles (sotanghon) and is topped with a rich, orange sauce made from shrimp and annatto. It historically originated near the old train stations of the city.

The Famous Tagaytay Bulalo

No trip to the highlands is complete without a steaming bowl of Bulalo. This beef marrow stew is made from fresh local beef and vegetables, perfected by the cool climate of the ridge.

Bibingka Samala

A specialty from Cavite City and Imus, this sticky rice cake is known for its distinct toasted top and creamy coconut texture. It has been a staple merienda for Caviteño families for generations.

Quesillo (Cavite White Cheese)

Commonly found in Gen. Trias, Quesillo is a fresh white cheese made from carabao's milk. It is best enjoyed with hot pandesal and is a testament to the province's rich dairy tradition.
