Ancestral Houses

Step back in time inside the Bonifacio Trial House and other preserved "Bahay na Bato" structures.

Bonifacio Trial House (Maragondon)

The very house where Andres Bonifacio, the Supremo of the Katipunan, was tried and convicted before his execution. A preserved structure of immense historical and emotional significance.

Aguinaldo Shrine (Kawit)

The ancestral home of General Emilio Aguinaldo is itself a grand example of late 19th-century Filipino-Spanish architecture. Its rooms have been preserved as a museum of the revolutionary era.

Bahay na Bato Structures (Bacoor & Cavite City)

Several municipalities still have preserved examples of the traditional "Bahay na Bato" (stone house) style. These two-storey homes with capiz shell windows represent a unique architectural heritage.

Cavite City Heritage Homes

The old districts of Cavite City harbor some of the most authentic and well-preserved colonial-era homes in the country, reflecting centuries of Spanish and American influence.
