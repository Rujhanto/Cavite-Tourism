Culinary Trails

From Pancit Estacion to Samala rice cakes, discover the unique heirloom recipes of Caviteño cuisine.

Pancit Estacion (Cavite City)

A unique local noodle dish that originated in Cavite City. Made with flat rice noodles, fresh seafood, and a distinct savory sauce, this is a must-try heirloom recipe that can't be found anywhere else.

Bulalo (Tagaytay)

Tagaytay's famous beef marrow soup is best enjoyed in the cool highland air. The Mahogany Market area is lined with Bulalo restaurants that have perfected this hearty classic over generations.

Buko Pie (Tagaytay)

The famous "Jacob's Buko Pie" from Tagaytay is a beloved pasalubong. Made with fresh young coconut in a flaky, buttery crust, it's the quintessential Cavite souvenir.

Samala Rice Cakes (Cavite City)

A traditional Caviteño rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar. These sweet treats are handmade by local families and sold at specialty shops and pasalubong centers.
