Historical Landmarks

Cavite is the "Historical Capital of the Philippines." Walk through the sites where our nation was born.

Aguinaldo Shrine (Kawit)

The ancestral home of General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first President of the Philippines. It was from the balcony of this house that the Philippine flag was first waved and the national anthem was first played on June 12, 1898.

Bonifacio Trial House (Maragondon)

The site where Andres Bonifacio, the Supremo of the Katipunan, was tried and sentenced to death. A sobering and deeply significant place for understanding the full complexity of the Philippine Revolution.

Corregidor Island

Known as "The Rock," Corregidor served as the last line of defense of the Philippines during World War II. The ruins, tunnels, and the famous Pacific War Memorial make it a must-visit for history buffs.

Gen. Trias Heritage Sites

Named after General Mariano Trias, this municipality holds multiple heritage sites including old Spanish-era structures and markers commemorating the revolutionary struggle for independence.
