Heritage Churches

Discover the centuries-old faith and intricate colonial architecture preserved in Cavite's sacred halls.

Imus Cathedral

Also known as the Cathedral of Our Lady of the Pillar, this structure features impressive Latin-cross architecture and red brick walls. It serves as a spiritual anchor and a symbol of the historic city of Imus.

Our Lady of the Assumption (Maragondon)

A National Cultural Treasure, this church is famous for its intricate, hand-carved wooden doors and retablo. It stands as one of the best-preserved examples of Jesuit colonial architecture in the Philippines.

Our Lady of Candelaria (Silang)

Known for its massive stone walls and historic belfry, the Silang Church is famous for its unique "Retablo Mayor," which features elaborate carvings that tell the story of the local faith and heritage.

St. Mary Magdalene Church (Kawit)

This historic church was where Emilio Aguinaldo was baptized. Its weathered facade and classic Baroque style reflect the deep intertwining of Philippine history and religious tradition.
